## Company Snapshot

SYSTEMMAG is a Paris-based French company focused on magnetic fastening, adjustment, and closure systems. The catalogue lists the company as:

- Legal form: SYSTEMMAG SAS
- Address: 20 rue Bouvier, 75011 Paris, France
- Phone: 01 45 08 91 41
- Email: contact@systemmag.com
- Website: www.systemmag.com

The company develops, manufactures, and markets high-performance magnetic fasteners using small articulated neodymium magnets and magnetic zippers. The products are positioned as alternatives to buttons, hooks, traditional zippers, Velcro-style hook-and-loop closures, clips, and laces.

The official site describes SYSTEMMAG as a magnetic closing and fastening system that adapts from shoes to bras, from ages 1 to 99. Their own framing is very functional: ease of use, fast closure, adjustability, and adaptation to many objects and body shapes.

## Origin Story And Timeline

The strongest brand story is founder-led and practical:

- 1997: Éric Sitbon imagines a magnetic fastening system after dealing with the slowness of opening a child's overall hooks.
- 1998: first patent filed for a device to hold, adjust, close, or fasten parts of garments, shoes, or accessories.
- 1999: Éric Sitbon and SYSTEMMAG win a French innovation competition organized by the Ministry of Research and ANVAR in the "project in emergence" category.
- 2000: Éric and Agnès Sitbon create SALOMÉ - SYSTEMMAG to develop and commercialize the invention; the company wins again in the "creation-development" category.
- 2001: recognized as an innovative company by the Île-de-France competition supported by BRED, Banque Populaire, and SPEF.
- 2002: second patent covering improvements including alternating polarity for stronger fastening in a compact format.
- 2003-2004: industrialization work with SALOME to design and deploy tools for serial production.
- 2005: MODAMONT presentation and development of magnetic zip product families.
- 2006: magnetic zip patent filed; SARL becomes SAS; Île de France Développement invests.
- 2007-2011: fourth patent around SCE, "Système catalyseur d'énergies."
- 2012: REACH compliance for components and manufacturing process.
- 2013: magnet strength range expanded to N38, N40, N45, N48; coating developed to resist 10,000 washes according to the official history page.
- 2014: approval obtained to supply US Army suppliers; N52 magnet strength added; SCE testing on tee shirts and medical tests with the Berck physiotherapy school.
- 2015-2016: SCE development for backpacks, garments, combat vests, biomagnetic exoskeletons, and extreme sports equipment.

Website implication: the new site should not bury the origin story. It gives the company human credibility and explains why the product matters: everyday closures are a real accessibility, speed, and ergonomics problem.

## Product Principle

SYSTEMMAG products use rare-earth neodymium-iron-boron magnets. The catalogue says the magnets are small, ultra-powerful ceramics, treated against corrosion/oxidation with passivated zinc coating, and arranged in articulated "magnet blocks" assembled with a flexible elastomer binder.

Key technical differentiators:

- Alternating polarities: small magnets are placed end to end so polarity alternates, giving much higher fastening power than a single larger magnet of equivalent volume.
- Articulation/flexibility: small magnets and elastic binder let the fastener follow curved or complex shapes, including the human body.
- Sliding adjustment: magnet blocks can move inside a sheath or pouch, allowing precise fit adjustment.
- Peel-open behavior: fasteners resist pulling/tension but can be opened by peeling the magnetic elements away progressively.
- Integration flexibility: magnets can be inserted into fabric pouches, special housings, strips, sheaths, and heat-sealed zip structures.
- Customization: spacing, length, magnet count, magnet grade, pouch material, and product format can be adapted to the application.

Patent US6954968B1 supports the core concept of a device for mutually adjusting or fixing parts of garments, shoes, or accessories using magnetic elements. The patent's listed prior-art date is 1998-12-03, inventor Éric Sitbon, publication date 2005-10-18.

## Product Families

### Magnet Strips

Small neodymium magnets placed end to end in one or two rows on a PU base with silicone glue. Users can cut strips to required length and integrate them into the final product. Available in multiple widths and strengths.

Website treatment: these need product cards with technical specs, integration guidance, and "request feasibility/cost" CTA.

### Magnet Blocks

Cut or prepared blocks made from magnet strips, supplied as compatible sets of two. The number of magnets and strip type determine fastening power. They are inserted into pouches, housings, or sheaths.

Website treatment: explain "compatible pair" visually. A simple polarity diagram would help nontechnical buyers understand why orientation matters.

### Straight Sheaths

Ready-to-stitch fabric sheaths/pouches for inserting magnet blocks. Used where magnets need to slide for adjustment. The catalogue stresses stitching zones and avoiding stitching through the central sliding area.

Website treatment: show sewing/integration diagrams and note that magnet blocks are sold/inserted separately.

### Shaped Sheaths

Ready-to-fit sheath sets with magnet blocks, originally developed for lingerie/bra fastening but adaptable to other uses. Usually one long sheath allows sliding/adjustment and a shorter counterpart forms the fastener.

Website treatment: useful for apparel and intimate-wear buyers. Needs tasteful, technical imagery rather than lifestyle-heavy presentation.

### C / CD / CE Zips

Long multipoint magnetic fasteners. C and CD zips use single magnets with configurable spacing. CE zips use magnet blocks and are suited for applications requiring more direct force, such as coats or jackets.

Website treatment: compare zip families by strength, spacing, configuration, and use case.

### G Zips

Magnetic zips with centered heat-sealed imprints. Can use single magnets or magnet blocks. The catalogue notes applications where a removable/shared/interchangeable part is useful, such as shoulder straps.

Website treatment: position as modular/removable fastening for bags, straps, apparel, and equipment.

## Certification, Compliance, And Safety

Claims from catalogue:

- OEKO-TEX Standard 100 class 2 certification.
- REACH compliance is mentioned in official timeline for 2012.
- Magnet coating is designed to resist corrosion/oxidation and outdoor conditions, including humidity and salt mist.
- Magnets retain characteristics up to 90°C; catalogue says up to 200°C may be available on request.
- Machine washing is possible, even at high temperatures, but fasteners should be closed before washing.
- Catalogue includes warning labels and handling recommendations because the products contain extremely powerful permanent magnets.

Safety and caution points:

- Avoid close contact with magnetic information carriers such as credit cards, magnetic tapes, floppy disks, magnetic tickets.
- Keep away from electronic devices, especially pacemakers and other medical equipment.
- Keep out of reach of children.
- Avoid ingestion or insertion into the body.
- Use correct magnet orientation; upside-down or offset blocks may still close but resistance is significantly reduced.
- Avoid stitching through central sliding areas.
- Use thin but mechanically resistant covering fabric; air gap reduces fastening power.

The catalogue reports an internal three-year study involving Professor Frank and 177 pacemaker patients under supervision of the CCPPRB of Pitié-Salpêtrière hospital, stating no undesirable effects were observed. For a public website, this should be handled carefully: mention only if the client can provide the study/reference and legal approval for the wording.

Website design direction:

- Industrial, precise, technical, and modern.

- Use macro product photography, technical diagrams, comparison tables, and application grids.
- Make CTAs practical: "Discuss an application", "Request feasibility and cost", "Download catalogue", "Contact engineering office".
- Prioritize trust signals: patents, innovation awards, OEKO-TEX, REACH, washing durability, R&D, manufacturing capability, Paris address.

## Positioning Summary

SYSTEMMAG should be positioned as a specialized French engineering and manufacturing company for patented magnetic fastening systems, not as a generic magnet supplier. Their differentiation is the full system: articulated magnet blocks, alternating polarity, sewable/integrable formats, custom magnetic zips, R&D support, and experience across apparel, accessibility, medical, military, luggage, and industrial uses.

Suggested concise positioning:

"SYSTEMMAG designs and manufactures patented magnetic fastening systems that make products faster to close, easier to adjust, and simpler to use."

## Risks And Constraints For Website Copy

- Do not overstate medical safety or pacemaker compatibility without client-approved evidence.
- Do not claim active certifications unless the client provides current certificates.
- Avoid implying patents are currently enforceable without a confirmed patent-status review.
- Avoid using "Velcro" generically in public copy unless legally acceptable; use "hook-and-loop" instead.
- Some catalogue English contains awkward translations and typos; rewrite copy professionally rather than copying it.
- Product references may be old; verify current SKU naming before building product tables.

## Immediate Build Implications

- Build a serious B2B technical site, not a trendy startup landing page.
- Use product images/technical diagrams as primary visuals.
- Bilingual support is likely important because current site is French and catalogue is English.
- The information architecture should support both product-led and application-led browsing.
- The contact path should be consultation-oriented because many products are custom or require feasibility review.
- Use clear comparison tables for zip/sheath/block families.
- Use micro-diagrams/animations for magnetic behavior: slide, align, close, peel, resist pull.
